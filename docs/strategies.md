## Grid Trading

### Description
Places buy and sell orders at regular intervals (grid) around a price level. Profits from price oscillations within the grid.

### How It Works
1. Set a price range (upper/lower bounds)
2. Divide range into grid levels
3. Place buy orders at each level below current price
4. Place sell orders at each level above current price
5. Each "cross" of a grid line = profit

### Best For
- Sideways/ranging markets
- High volatility pairs
- Crypto (especially altcoins)

### Pros
- Consistent small profits
- Market-neutral (works in any direction)
- Automated, low maintenance

### Cons
- Capital tied up in many orders
- Loses in strong trends
- Requires adjustment of grid parameters

### Risk Level: Medium

## DCA (Dollar-Cost Averaging)

### Description
Buying an asset incrementally at regular intervals, regardless of price. Reduces average entry cost over time.

### How It Works
1. Set investment amount per purchase
2. Set interval (daily, weekly, monthly)
3. Automatically buy at set times
4. Hold long-term

### Best For
- Long-term investors
- Bitcoin/Ethereum
- Volatile assets you believe in

### Pros
- Reduces timing risk
- Emotionless investing
- Easy to automate
- Good for passive income

### Cons
- Requires long holding period
- Doesn't protect from major crashes
- Slow to profit

### Risk Level: Low-Medium

## Momentum Trading

### Description
Buying assets that are trending up, selling when momentum fades. Captures "hot" moves.

### How It Works
1. Identify strong trending assets (RSI, MACD, moving averages)
2. Enter when momentum confirms direction
3. Exit when momentum weakens
4. Use stop-loss for protection

### Best For
- Trending markets
- Strong directional moves
- Medium-term holds (days to weeks)

### Pros
- High profit potential in trends
- Clear entry/exit signals
- Works on any timeframe

### Cons
- False breakouts
- Requires good timing
- Can miss reversals

### Risk Level: High

---

## Mean Reversion

### Description
Assumes price will return to its average. Buy oversold, sell overbought.

### How It Works
1. Calculate moving average (SMA, EMA)
2. Identify when price deviates significantly
3. Buy when oversold, sell when overbought
4. Use Bollinger Bands, RSI for signals

### Best For
- Ranging markets
- Pairs with known price ranges
- Pairs with high mean reversion tendency

### Pros
- Clear buy/sell signals
- Works well in sideways markets
- Good risk/reward ratios

### Cons
- Explosive moves can wipe out accounts
- Hard to determine "significant" deviation
- Requires good volatility filtering

### Risk Level: Medium-High

## Scalping

### Description
Making many small trades, holding positions for seconds to minutes. Targets tiny price movements.

### How It Works
1. Use high leverage
2. Target 0.1-1% moves
3. High volume of trades
4. Tight stop-losses
5. Quick execution required

### Best For
- High-liquidity markets
- Low-spread pairs
- Experienced traders

### Pros
- Can generate consistent daily profits
- Lower per-trade risk
- Many opportunities

### Cons
- Requires fast execution
- High fees (many trades)
- Stressful
- Not suitable for beginners

### Risk Level: Very High

---

## Swing Trading

### Description
Holding positions for days to weeks, capturing medium-term price swings.

### How It Works
1. Identify potential swing setups
2. Enter on pullbacks in trend
3. Hold until target reached
4. Use technical analysis for entries/exits

### Best For
- Part-time traders
- Medium-term timeframes
- Any trending market

### Pros
- Less time-intensive than scalping
- Good profit potential
- Can combine with day job

### Cons
- Overnight exposure
- Requires patience
- Can miss short-term opportunities

### Risk Level: Medium

---

## Trend Following

### Description
Buy assets in uptrend, sell in downtrend. "The trend is your friend."

### How It Works
1. Identify trend direction (moving averages, trendlines)
2. Enter on pullbacks in direction of trend
3. Exit when trend reverses
4. Use trailing stops

### Best For
- Strong trending markets
- Long-term traders
- Multiple asset classes

### Pros
- Unlimited profit potential
- Clear rules
- Works on any timeframe

### Cons
- Many small losses
- Requires discipline
- Can be wrong for long periods

### Risk Level: Medium

---

## Arbitrage

### Description
Exploiting price differences between markets or exchanges simultaneously.

### How It Works
1. Monitor prices on multiple exchanges
2. Buy where cheap, sell where expensive
3. Execute both sides nearly simultaneously
4. Profit from spread

### Best For
- High-liquidity assets
- Experienced developers
- Pairs with price discrepancies

### Pros
- Low risk (market-neutral)
- Consistent profits
- Works in any market direction

### Cons
- Requires fast execution
- Small profit margins
- High competition
- Capital intensive
- Fees eat profits

### Risk Level: Low (execution risk high)

---

## Market Making

### Description
Providing liquidity by placing limit orders on both sides. Earn from spread.

### How It Works
1. Place buy limit order below market
2. Place sell limit order above market
3. When either fills, hedge on other exchange
4. Profit from spread

### Best For
- Institutional traders
- High-volume traders
- Stable markets

### Pros
- Consistent small profits
- Low directional risk
- Fees from exchange rebates

### Cons
- Requires large capital
- Adverse selection risk
- Complex to implement
- Regulatory considerations

### Risk Level: Medium (inventory risk high)

## Getting Started Recommendations

### Beginner
1. **DCA** - Start with small amounts, learn holding
2. **Grid** - Good for sideways markets, automated

### Intermediate
3. **Swing Trading** - Combine with trend following
4. **Momentum** - Higher rewards, more active

### Advanced
5. **Scalping** - High stress, high capital
6. **Arbitrage** - Technical, capital intensive
7. **Market Making** - Institutional level

## Important Notes

- **No strategy is guaranteed profitable**
- Backtest extensively before live trading
- Start with paper trading
- Never risk more than you can afford to lose
- Consider fees/spreads in calculations
- Diversify strategies, don't put all eggs in one basket
